Chichen Itza, located on the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico, is a renowned archaeological site representing the ancient Maya civilization's architectural prowess and astronomical achievements. This UNESCO World Heritage site is famous for its impressive pyramid, El Castillo, which served both as a temple and a calendar. The site also features a massive ball court, sacrificial cenotes, and intricate carvings, showcasing the Maya's advanced understanding of mathematics and celestial events.
